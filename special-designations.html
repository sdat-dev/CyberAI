Special Designations

The University at Albany is proud to be a member of the National Centers of Academic Excellence in Cyber Defense Program, sponsored by the National Security Agency (NSA) and the Department of Homeland Security (DHS).

Under this program, the University at Albany has received the following designations:

  • National Centers of Academic Excellence in Cyber Defense Education (CAE-CDE: 2018-2023)
  • National Centers of Academic Excellence in Cyber Defense Research (CAE-R: 2017-2022)

These designations are designed to recognize Universities and programs that are best equipped to educate students on cyber defense technology and security and learn the skills necessary to prevent against a cyber attack.

The CAE-CDE designation denotes that the University at Albany has an outstanding program in Cyber Defense education, which will help prepare students to work in the cybersecurity field.

The CAE-R designation denotes that the University at Albany has an active research center that focuses on Cyber Defense, with research activities incorporated into courses and degree programs.

The University at Albany is one of 301 Universities and Colleges in the United States that has been awarded either a CAE-CDE or CAE-R designation, one of only 15 in the State of New York, and one of only 44 institutions in the United States that have received both designations.
