ABOUT THE NYRDC & WORKSHOP: 

The New York Federal Statistical Research Data Center (NYRDC) is part of the Federal Statistical Research Data Center (FSRDC) Network. The FSRDCs are secure facilities managed by the Census Bureau that provide access to a range of restricted-use microdata from agencies such as the U.S. Census Bureau, the National Center for Health Statistics, and the Bureau of Labor Statistics.  The NYRDC operates three labs - one at Baruch College-CUNY, one at Cornell University, and one at Yale University.  Researchers must have an approved project before conducting research at an FSRDC. 

 

The workshop at UAlbany will:

Provide examples of the types of questions that can be addressed using FSRDC data

Describe the restricted-access demographic, economic, health and other data available through the FSRDCs

Explain the process of applying to use the NYRDC labs

The NYRDC Workshop will be led by:

  Diane Gibson, Ph.D., Executive Director of the NYRDC-Baruch & Associate Professor at the Marxe School of Public and International Affairs at Baruch College-CUNY

  Shirley Liu, Ph.D., Administrator of the NYRDC-Baruch

The University at Albany is a member of the NYRDC Consortium.  Membership in the NYRDC Consortium covers access fees for faculty members, graduate students, and researchers affiliated with member institutions. 

 

To learn more about the NYRDC, you can visit: https://nyrdc.cornell.edu
