About

The Divisions for Research and Academic Affairs are pleased to announce a new university-wide initiative to promote interdisciplinary team research and infrastructure enhancement with a focus at the intersection of two strategic areas: Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Cyber Security (CS). The initiative is supported by UAlbany’s Accelerator funding allocated to projects which seek to implement the University’s Strategic Plan recently developed undccer the leadership of President Havidán Rodríguez.

The overarching aim of AICSI is to position UAlbany as a leader in the application of Artificial Intelligence in Cyber Security.

This initiative’s short-term goal is to:
Accelerate research in AI and CS by creating interdisciplinary teams that will compete for federal funding in these strategic areas.

This initiative’s long-term goal is to:
Catalyze the creation of an Institute for Cybersecurity and Artificial Intelligence.

These attainments will contribute to UAlbany’s rise in national rankings, e.g., in NSF’s Higher Education Research and Development expenditures, Carnegie Classifications of Institutions of Higher Education, and US News and World Reports and recognition within the SUNY System as an epicenter for AI and CS.

Artificial Intelligence is rapidly permeating Transportation, Communication, Healthcare, Medicine, Finance, Weather Forecasting, Defense, Manufacturing, and practically every field of human endeavor. AI already plays a significant role in transforming all aspects of life as we know it today and CS science creates knowledge and devices that underpin how that infrastructure is secure and trusted. Implementation of AI as well as basic research to further develop and improve AI and Cyberdefense is among the top priorities of essentially all national and regional organizations, especially those responsible for promoting and advancing research, education, and workforce training. Significant funds are being earmarked for research, development, and training in the fields of AI and CS. Owing to the importance of AI and UAlbany’s significant assets and distributed expertise across the three campuses, UAlbany has selected AI and CS as strategic areas for growth.

A typical implementation of AI requires continual and secure acquisition of large data from distributed sensors and detectors. The data are then processed to reveal certain patterns and arrive at specific decisions followed by actions based on the algorithm used to train the AI system. AI makes extensive use of secure networks in the collection of information, data transport and storage, processing, and communication of the outcomes to various end-users and end-nodes. Accordingly, security is imperative at various levels and stages, and physical locations to maintain the integrity of the AI system. It is paramount to have secure and rugged infrastructure in place to safeguard against malicious actors.

Secure cyber infrastructures require physically secure computer networks, data vaults, and servers that are hardened against intrusion. The use of encryption and multifactor authentication, early and predictive detection, neutralizing impending threats using AI, and security of/from the cloud are essential. Mitigating human errors and automated and coordinated rapid response to incidents of security violations are essential components of a secure cyber infrastructure.

UAlbany is home to our nation’s first College of Emergency Preparedness, Homeland Security, and Cyber Security (CEHC). UAlbany’s expertise in AI and Cyber Security (CS) resides in CEHC, the College of Engineering and Applied Sciences, the College of Arts and Sciences and the School of Business. UAlbany researchers are known at the national and international levels for their expertise and contribution to the field. They have engaged federal agencies and leadership, and have given expert testimony to congressional committees to assist them in formulating sound policies and plans to strengthen national cyber infrastructure and cyber security. UAlbany's infrastructure includes secure data server capability and high-performance computing clusters which are extensively used by researchers.

This Artificial Intelligence and Cyber Security Initiative (AICSI) leverages UAlbany’s faculty expertise in the formation of teams in and at the interface of the two priority areas. Participating UAlbany scholars will collectively identify a high-value project, develop a viable plan and methodology, and pursue extramural federal support. UAlbany will provide up to $25,000 to support select groups that develop outstanding white papers to assist them in the preparation of extramural funding proposal(s). Teams may aggregate their funding to increase impact as long as there is no overlap in white papers (Think Institute!).
